Fake Token: Most Common, Least Effective

Zero-value: High ROI Attack Vector
Only 30.58% of attempts, but responsible for 50.33% of successful cases. Free to execute (no token cost), making it highly profitable.

How Address Poisoning Works

Attackers exploit transaction history UI patterns to trick victims into sending funds to lookalike addresses

1

Target Identification

Attacker monitors victim's wallet for regular transaction patterns and recipients

2

Address Generation

Creates lookalike address with matching first/last characters (vanity address)

3

History Poisoning

Sends zero/tiny value transactions to plant fake address in victim's history

4

Victim Confusion

Victim copies wrong address from history, sending funds to attacker
